The unique features of a Kerala Christian Wedding

Kettukuri

This is the official invitation given by the local church of the groom which is to be handed over to the local church of the bride.

Athirpootha

The headquarters of the Archbishop administers preparation for the wedding course to the bride and the groom.

Manasammadam

The willingness or the assent of the couple is taken to get wedded on a day other than a Friday

Kurbana

Prior to taking the consent of the couple, a small prayer is conducted by the church which is called Kurbana.

Madhuramvekal

The pre-wedding preparations where the groom shaves, has a haircut and an oil bath and sits under an umbrella held by his brother-in-law. The bride too prepares herself with an oil bath following which she is dressed in a beautiful saree by her sister-in-law.

Mantra Kodi

As per the custom, during the nuptials the groom gifts a new sari to his wife. This sari is called the ‘mantra kodi’

Minnukettu

An evening before the wedding seven threads are taken from the mantra kodi and tied to a leaf-shaped gold pendant inscribed with a cross. This minnu is placed on the bride’s head by the priest and then tied around the bride’s neck by the groom.

Outfit

For the wedding ceremony, the bride wears an off-white silk sari with a wide border. Her sari ‘pallu’ acts as a veil or she wears a separate veil over her head.

Christian Wedding- The Vows and Rituals

A Christian Wedding is one of the popular weddings worldwide. Getting married in a church with the blessings of Christ would surely make the newlyweds a heavenly made couple. Some of the rituals and ceremonies a typical Christian Wedding has would be

Pre wedding

Some distinctive pre wedding rituals in a Christian wedding include the engagement ceremony. This is followed by a bridal shower (hen party) where the bride and her female friends gather at the bride’s place and rejoice by singing and dancing. Similarly the groom celebrates the Bachelor’s party with his male friends.

Wedding

The groom sends a car for the bride’s pick up on the wedding day. The bride is walked down the aisle of the church (or wherever the venue is set up) by her father. The groom awaits the bride and her father at the end of aisle. The father hands over the bride’s hand to the groom. Thereafter the priest reads psalms from the Hoy Bible and asks the bride and the groom to confirm their consent for the marriage. Once each of them says “I do” they exchange rings and the couple is thus declared married by the priest’s announcement of ‘I now declare you husband and wife’

Post Wedding

Immediately after the wedding there is a reception party which is usually held at the same spot. This a grand affair where there is cake cutting, wine drinking, toast made by the toastmaster (the best man) in honor of the couple and a lot of music and dancing.
